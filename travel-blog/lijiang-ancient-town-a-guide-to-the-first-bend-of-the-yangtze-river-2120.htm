Lijiang Ancient Town: A Guide to the First Bend of the Yangtze River

Nestled in the heart of Yunnan Province, Lijiang Ancient Town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that effortlessly blends history, culture, and breathtaking natural scenery. Among its many wonders, the First Bend of the Yangtze River stands out as a must-see attraction. This guide will take you through the highlights of Lijiang and its surrounding gems, offering insider tips for an unforgettable journey.

Why Lijiang Ancient Town is a Traveler’s Dream

Lijiang’s charm lies in its well-preserved Naxi architecture, cobblestone streets, and vibrant local culture. The town is divided into three main sections: Dayan Old Town, Shuhe Ancient Town, and Baisha Village, each offering unique experiences.

Dayan Old Town: The Heart of Lijiang

Wander through narrow alleys lined with traditional wooden houses, where the scent of yak butter tea lingers in the air. Key spots include:
- Mufu Palace: A restored mansion showcasing Naxi royal history.
- Sifang Street: The bustling central square, perfect for people-watching.
- Black Dragon Pool: A serene park with a postcard-perfect view of Jade Dragon Snow Mountain.

Shuhe Ancient Town: A Quieter Alternative

If you prefer a more relaxed vibe, Shuhe offers charming tea houses and artisan workshops. Don’t miss:
- Tea Horse Road Museum: Learn about the ancient trade route.
- Local Handicrafts: Pick up handmade silver jewelry or Naxi embroidery.

Baisha Village: Where Tradition Thrives

This lesser-visited gem is home to Baisha Murals, a fusion of Han, Tibetan, and Naxi art styles.

The First Bend of the Yangtze River: A Natural Wonder

Just a short drive from Lijiang, the First Bend of the Yangtze River (known as Shigu in Chinese) is where the mighty river takes a dramatic 180-degree turn. This geological marvel is not only a photographer’s paradise but also a site of historical significance.

How to Get There

  • By Car: A 2-hour scenic drive from Lijiang.
  • Guided Tours: Many operators include stops at Tiger Leaping Gorge for a full-day adventure.

What to Do at the First Bend

  1. View from Shigu Town: Climb the hilltop pavilion for panoramic vistas.
  2. Iron Chain Bridge: Walk across this historic bridge, built during the Ming Dynasty.
  3. Local Naxi Culture: Visit nearby villages to see traditional farming methods.

Beyond the First Bend: Nearby Attractions

Tiger Leaping Gorge

One of Asia’s deepest gorges, this hiking hotspot offers thrilling trails with jaw-dropping views. Choose between:
- Upper Trail: Easier, with accessible viewpoints.
- Lower Trail: A challenging hike for adventure seekers.

Jade Dragon Snow Mountain

A sacred peak for the Naxi people, this mountain is a year-round destination. Highlights include:
- Glacier Park: Ride a cable car to 4,500 meters for snow-covered vistas.
- Impression Lijiang Show: A cultural performance directed by Zhang Yimou.

Travel Tips for Your Lijiang Adventure

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (March-May): Mild weather and blooming flowers.
  • Autumn (September-November): Clear skies and fewer crowds.

Where to Stay

  • Boutique Guesthouses: Experience Naxi hospitality in Dayan Old Town.
  • Luxury Resorts: For stunning mountain views, consider accommodations near Jade Dragon Snow Mountain.

Local Cuisine to Try

  • Naxi Baba: A savory or sweet flatbread.
  • Yak Meat Hotpot: A hearty dish perfect for chilly evenings.
  • Lijiang Rice Noodles: A breakfast staple served with flavorful broth.

Sustainable Travel in Lijiang

As tourism grows, it’s crucial to support eco-friendly practices:
- Respect Local Customs: Ask before photographing Naxi ceremonies.
- Shop Responsibly: Buy from local artisans rather than mass-produced souvenirs.
- Minimize Plastic Use: Carry a reusable water bottle—many guesthouses offer refills.

Lijiang Ancient Town and the First Bend of the Yangtze River are more than just destinations; they’re gateways to a world where history, culture, and nature intertwine. Whether you’re exploring ancient streets or marveling at the power of the Yangtze, every moment here is a step into something extraordinary.
