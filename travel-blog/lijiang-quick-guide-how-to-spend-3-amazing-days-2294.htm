Lijiang Quick Guide: How to Spend 3 Amazing Days

Nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas, Lijiang is a dreamy destination that blends ancient charm with breathtaking landscapes. Whether you're a history buff, an adventure seeker, or just looking to unwind, this UNESCO-listed gem has something for everyone. Here’s how to make the most of three unforgettable days in Lijiang.

Day 1: Exploring the Old Town & Naxi Culture

Morning: Wander Through Lijiang Old Town

Start your day in the heart of Lijiang—the Old Town. With its cobblestone streets, wooden architecture, and canals, it feels like stepping into a painting. Don’t miss Sifang Street, the central square buzzing with cafes, souvenir shops, and street performers. Climb up to Lion Hill for a panoramic view of the town’s iconic tiled rooftops.

Afternoon: Discover Naxi Heritage

Head to the Dongba Culture Museum to learn about the Naxi people’s fascinating Dongba script, one of the world’s last living pictographic languages. Then, visit Mu Family Mansion, the former residence of Lijiang’s rulers, showcasing traditional Naxi architecture and gardens.

Evening: Savor Local Flavors

As the sun sets, dive into Lijiang’s food scene. Try Naxi Baba (a savory or sweet flatbread) or Yak meat hotpot at a local eatery. End the night with a stroll along the illuminated canals—pure magic!

Day 2: Nature & Adventure at Jade Dragon Snow Mountain

Morning: Sunrise at Jade Dragon Snow Mountain

Rise early for a day trip to Jade Dragon Snow Mountain (Yulong Xueshan). The snow-capped peaks glow at dawn—perfect for photos. Take the cable car up to Glacier Park for jaw-dropping views at 4,680 meters. Pro tip: Rent a jacket; it’s chilly up there!

Afternoon: Blue Moon Valley & Impression Lijiang Show

Descend to Blue Moon Valley, where turquoise pools mirror the mountain. Walk the wooden boardwalks or hop on an electric cart to explore. Later, catch the Impression Lijiang show—a stunning outdoor performance directed by Zhang Yimou, featuring hundreds of local performers against the mountain backdrop.

Evening: Relax in Shuhe Ancient Town

On your way back, stop at Shuhe, a quieter cousin of Lijiang Old Town. Wander its lanes, sip Yunnan coffee, or unwind at a riverside teahouse.

Day 3: Day Trips & Hidden Gems

Morning: Tiger Leaping Gorge Excursion

Adventure lovers, this is for you! Drive 2 hours to Tiger Leaping Gorge, one of Asia’s deepest canyons. Hike the upper trail for epic views of the roaring Jinsha River. If short on time, visit the Middle Gorge for a quick but thrilling walk.

Afternoon: Baisha Village & Local Crafts

Return to Lijiang and detour to Baisha Village, the original Naxi capital. Visit Baisha Murals, a mix of Buddhist, Taoist, and Tibetan art. Then, drop by a local embroidery workshop—Naxi handicrafts make unique souvenirs.

Evening: Sunset at Black Dragon Pool

Wrap up your trip at Black Dragon Pool Park. The iconic view of Jade Dragon Snow Mountain reflected in the pool is postcard-perfect, especially at golden hour. Grab a drink at a nearby café and soak it all in.

Extra Tips for Your Lijiang Trip

  • Altitude prep: Lijiang sits at 2,400m—stay hydrated and take it slow if you’re sensitive.
  • Best time to visit: Spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) offer mild weather and fewer crowds.
  • Transport: Taxis are cheap, but walking is the best way to explore the Old Town.

Three days in Lijiang will leave you enchanted—whether it’s the culture, the landscapes, or the warm Naxi hospitality. Pack your bags and get ready for an adventure!
