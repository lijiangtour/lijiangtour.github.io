How to Get to Lijiang Ancient Town: Travel Tips

Lijiang Ancient Town, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is one of China’s most enchanting destinations. With its cobblestone streets, traditional Naxi architecture, and stunning mountain backdrops, it’s no wonder travelers flock here year-round. But before you immerse yourself in its charm, you’ll need to figure out the best way to get there. Whether you’re coming from within China or abroad, here’s everything you need to know about reaching Lijiang Ancient Town.

Getting to Lijiang: Transportation Options

By Air: Lijiang Sanyi International Airport

The fastest way to reach Lijiang is by flying into Lijiang Sanyi International Airport (LJG). This airport connects to major Chinese cities like Beijing, Shanghai, Chengdu, and Kunming, as well as a few international routes.

What to Expect:

  • From Kunming: A 1-hour flight; multiple daily departures.
  • From Beijing/Shanghai: Around 3-4 hours.
  • Airport to Town: Taxis (30-40 minutes, ~¥100) or airport shuttle buses (~¥20) are available.

Pro Tip: Book flights in advance during peak seasons (spring and autumn) for better prices.

By Train: Scenic Rail Journeys

For those who prefer trains, Lijiang is well-connected via China’s extensive rail network. The Lijiang Railway Station serves high-speed and overnight trains.

Popular Routes:

  • Kunming to Lijiang: High-speed trains take ~3 hours; regular trains take ~8-9 hours.
  • Dali to Lijiang: ~2 hours by high-speed rail, offering stunning countryside views.

Pro Tip: Opt for a daytime train to enjoy the picturesque Yunnan landscapes.

By Bus: Budget-Friendly but Longer

Long-distance buses are an option if you’re coming from nearby cities like Dali or Shangri-La. While cheaper, travel times can be lengthy due to mountain roads.

Key Bus Routes:

  • Dali to Lijiang: ~3-4 hours.
  • Shangri-La to Lijiang: ~4-5 hours (scenic but winding roads).

Pro Tip: Bring motion sickness pills if you’re prone to car sickness on mountain routes.

Navigating Lijiang Ancient Town

Once you arrive in Lijiang, getting around the ancient town is easy—and part of the adventure.

Walking: The Best Way to Explore

The town’s compact layout makes it perfect for walking. Cobblestone alleys, canals, and historic buildings are best explored on foot.

Must-See Spots:
- Sifang Street (Square Street): The bustling heart of the town.
- Mu Family Mansion: A glimpse into Naxi royalty.
- Black Dragon Pool: Iconic views of Jade Dragon Snow Mountain.

Bicycle Rentals

For a quicker way to cover ground, rent a bike (~¥30-50/day). Many guesthouses offer rentals, and cycling to nearby villages like Shuhe is a great day trip.

Taxis and Ride-Hailing

Taxis are available but can’t enter the ancient town’s core. Use them for trips to nearby attractions like Jade Dragon Snow Mountain or Blue Moon Valley.

Pro Tip: Didi (China’s Uber) works here—download the app before arrival.

Best Time to Visit Lijiang

Timing your trip can make all the difference in your experience.

Peak Season (April–October)

  • Pros: Warm weather, vibrant festivals (e.g., Torch Festival in July).
  • Cons: Crowds, higher prices.

Off-Season (November–March)

  • Pros: Fewer tourists, cheaper accommodations.
  • Cons: Chilly mornings and evenings; some attractions may close early.

Pro Tip: Visit in late autumn for golden foliage and mild weather.

Where to Stay in Lijiang

Choosing the right accommodation enhances your trip. Here are the best areas:

Inside the Ancient Town

  • Pros: Immersive experience, easy access to sights.
  • Cons: Can be noisy at night; luggage restrictions (cobblestones make rolling suitcases tricky).

Shuhe Ancient Town

  • A quieter alternative with boutique hotels and cafes.

New Town (Xin Cheng)

  • More modern amenities, closer to transport hubs.

Pro Tip: Book a guesthouse with a rooftop terrace for epic mountain views.

Must-Try Local Experiences

Beyond sightseeing, dive into Lijiang’s culture:

Naxi Music Performances

Attend a traditional Dongjing Music show—an ancient art form preserved by the Naxi people.

Tea Horse Road Exploration

Learn about the historic trade route at the Tea Horse Road Museum.

Yak Meat Hot Pot

Warm up with a local specialty—spicy yak meat hot pot is a must-try!

Pro Tip: Visit a local market for fresh Yunnan coffee and wild mushroom dishes.

Final Travel Hacks

  • Altitude Awareness: Lijiang sits at ~2,400m. Stay hydrated to avoid altitude sickness.
  • Cash & Apps: Some small shops don’t take cards; have WeChat Pay/Alipay set up.
  • Respect Local Customs: The Naxi are matriarchal—avoid intrusive questions about their culture.

Now that you’re equipped with these tips, all that’s left is to pack your bags and set off for Lijiang’s timeless beauty!
